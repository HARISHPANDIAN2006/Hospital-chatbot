# Patient FAQ - Apollo Medicity Hospital

## Appointment & Consultation

### How do I book an appointment?

**Online Booking:**
1. Visit www.apollomedicity.in/book-appointment
2. Select department and doctor
3. Choose available time slot
4. Fill patient details
5. Receive confirmation via SMS and email

**Phone Booking:**
- Call: +91-44-2815-2000
- Available: Monday to Saturday, 8:00 AM - 8:00 PM

**Walk-in:**
- Visit registration counter at hospital
- Available during OPD hours

**Mobile App:**
- Download "Apollo Patient" app from Play Store/App Store
- Book and manage appointments

### What documents do I need for first visit?

- Valid government-issued ID (Aadhaar/PAN/Driving License/Passport)
- Previous medical records (if any)
- Previous prescriptions
- Insurance card (if applicable)
- Referral letter (if coming from another doctor)

### Can I choose a specific doctor?

Yes, you can select your preferred doctor while booking. However, availability depends on the doctor's schedule and slot availability.

### What if I need to cancel or reschedule?

- Cancel/reschedule at least 4 hours before appointment
- Online: Through website or app
- Phone: Call appointment desk at +91-44-2815-2000
- No-show policy: Multiple no-shows may require advance payment for future bookings

### What is the average waiting time?

- With appointment: 15-30 minutes
- Walk-in: 1-3 hours depending on department and rush
- Emergency: Immediate triage and attention

### Do you offer telemedicine consultations?

Yes, we offer video consultations for:
- Follow-up visits
- Prescription refills
- Minor health concerns
- Mental health consultations
- Chronic disease management

**Not suitable for:**
- First-time diagnosis
- Emergency situations
- Physical examinations

## Hospital Admission

### How do I get admitted?

**Planned Admission:**
1. Doctor recommends admission
2. Visit admission counter with:
   - Doctor's admission note
   - ID proof
   - Insurance card (if applicable)
3. Complete admission formalities
4. Advance deposit required (varies by treatment)

**Emergency Admission:**
- Report directly to Emergency Department
- Admission formalities completed by attendants later

### What documents are needed for admission?

**Mandatory:**
- Valid government ID
- Doctor's admission note/prescription
- Insurance pre-authorization (if using insurance)

**Additional:**
- Previous medical records
- Blood group card
- Consent forms (provided by hospital)

### How much advance deposit is required?

Varies by:
- Type of room (general/semi-private/private/suite)
- Estimated treatment cost
- Insurance coverage

**Typical ranges:**
- General ward: ₹10,000 - ₹20,000
- Private room: ₹25,000 - ₹50,000
- ICU: ₹50,000 - ₹1,00,000

**Note:** Insurance patients may have reduced or zero deposit with pre-authorization.

### Can I choose my room type?

Yes, subject to availability:
- General Ward (4-6 beds)
- Semi-Private (2 beds)
- Private Room (single bed)
- Deluxe Room
- Executive Suite

### What items should I bring for admission?

**Essential:**
- Comfortable clothes (2-3 sets)
- Personal toiletries
- Slippers/comfortable footwear
- Mobile phone and charger
- Important documents in a folder

**Do NOT bring:**
- Valuables or jewelry
- Large amounts of cash
- Outside food (unless permitted by doctor)

### Can attendants stay with the patient?

- General/Semi-private: One attendant allowed (no bed provided)
- Private rooms: One attendant with bed facility
- ICU/CCU: Attendants not allowed inside, waiting area available
- Pediatric ward: One parent can stay

## Discharge & Billing

### How does the discharge process work?

1. Doctor gives discharge orders (usually morning rounds)
2. Nursing staff prepares discharge summary
3. Visit billing counter for final bill
4. Complete payment/insurance settlement
5. Collect discharge summary, prescriptions, reports
6. Pharmacy provides take-home medicines
7. Counseling on post-discharge care (if needed)

**Average discharge time:** 2-4 hours from doctor's order

### What is included in discharge documents?

- Discharge summary with diagnosis
- List of procedures performed
- Medications to continue at home
- Follow-up appointment details
- Dos and don'ts
- Diet recommendations
- Reports and test results
- Fitness certificate (if applicable)

### How do I get my final bill?

**For cash patients:**
- Visit billing counter
- Final bill prepared after discharge order
- Make payment and collect receipt

**For insurance patients:**
- Insurance desk handles claim settlement
- May need to pay non-covered items
- Claim processing takes 7-15 days
- Copy of claim documents provided

### What payment methods are accepted?

- Cash
- Credit/Debit cards (all major cards)
- UPI (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm)
- Net Banking
- Health insurance (cashless or reimbursement)
- Corporate tie-ups
- Payment plans (for high-value treatments)

### Can I get an installment plan?

Yes, for treatments exceeding ₹1,00,000:
- Apply at billing counter
- Credit check may be required
- Interest rates vary
- Minimum 3 months, maximum 24 months
- Partner: Healthcare financing companies

### How do I get a copy of my medical records?

**Online:**
- Login to patient portal
- Download reports and summaries

**Offline:**
- Visit Medical Records Department (Ground Floor)
- Fill request form with ID proof
- Fee: ₹100 per record
- Collection: 24-48 hours

**For legal/insurance purposes:**
- Sealed copy: ₹500
- Notarized copy: ₹1,000
- Processing time: 3-5 working days

## Insurance & Payment

### Which insurance companies do you accept?

We have cashless tie-ups with 150+ insurance companies including:
- Star Health
- HDFC ERGO
- ICICI Lombard
- Max Bupa
- Care Health
- National Insurance
- New India Assurance
- All government schemes (CGHS, ESI, EHS)

**Full list:** www.apollomedicity.in/insurance

### How does cashless treatment work?

1. Inform hospital at admission that you have insurance
2. Provide insurance card and policy details
3. Hospital sends pre-authorization request to insurance company
4. Insurance approves covered amount (usually within 2-4 hours)
5. Treatment proceeds
6. Hospital settles bill directly with insurance
7. You pay only non-covered items (co-pay, exclusions, etc.)

### What if insurance doesn't cover the entire bill?

You will be responsible for:
- Items not covered under your policy
- Amount exceeding sum insured
- Co-payment (if applicable)
- Deductibles
- Room rent difference (if you choose higher category)

### Do you accept government health schemes?

Yes, we accept:
- CGHS (Central Government Health Scheme)
- ESI (Employee State Insurance)
- ECHS (Ex-Servicemen Contributory Health Scheme)
- State government schemes (PM-JAY, Chief Minister's Health Scheme)
- RSBY (Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana)

**Process:**
- Bring your scheme card
- Authorization obtained before treatment
- Cashless facility available

### Can I get a detailed bill breakdown?

Yes, detailed bills showing:
- Room charges (per day)
- Doctor consultation fees
- Procedure/surgery charges
- Medicines and consumables
- Diagnostic tests
- OT charges
- ICU charges (if applicable)
- Miscellaneous charges

Request at billing counter or patient portal.

## Emergency Services

### When should I visit the emergency department?

**Immediately visit for:**
- Chest pain or pressure
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Severe bleeding
- Sudden severe headache
- Unconsciousness or altered mental state
- Seizures
- Suspected stroke (facial drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
- Severe injuries from accidents
- High fever with altered consciousness
- Severe allergic reactions
- Suspected heart attack
- Poisoning or overdose

### Is emergency service available 24/7?

Yes, our Emergency Department operates 24 hours a day, 365 days a year with:
- Emergency physicians on duty
- Immediate access to all specialists
- 24/7 diagnostic services (lab, imaging)
- Ambulance services
- Trauma care team

### Do I need an appointment for emergency?

No appointment needed. Walk in directly to Emergency Department.

### What is the triage process?

Upon arrival:
1. Quick assessment by triage nurse
2. Patients categorized by severity:
   - **Critical** (Red): Immediate attention
   - **Urgent** (Yellow): Seen within 30 minutes
   - **Non-urgent** (Green): Seen within 1-2 hours
3. Treatment prioritized based on medical urgency, not arrival time

### Do you have ambulance services?

Yes:
- **Emergency ambulances:** Advanced Life Support (ALS) equipped
- **Non-emergency patient transport:** For transfers, appointments
- **Booking:** Call +91-44-2815-2222
- **Coverage:** Within 50 km radius
- **Charges:** Based on distance and type of service
