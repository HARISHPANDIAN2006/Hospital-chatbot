# Hospital Policies - Apollo Medicity Hospital

## Patient Rights & Responsibilities

### Patient Rights

As a patient at Apollo Medicity Hospital, you have the right to:

1. **Respectful Care**
   - Be treated with dignity, respect, and consideration
   - Receive care regardless of race, religion, gender, age, disability, or socioeconomic status
   - Be addressed by proper name and title
   - Have cultural and spiritual beliefs respected

2. **Privacy & Confidentiality**
   - Privacy during medical treatment, examination, and consultation
   - Confidentiality of all medical records and communications
   - Private conversations with healthcare providers
   - Visitors only with your permission (except in emergency)

3. **Information & Communication**
   - Be informed about your diagnosis, treatment options, and prognosis in understandable language
   - Know the names and roles of healthcare providers caring for you
   - Receive information about the benefits, risks, and costs of treatment
   - Access to interpreters if needed
   - Access to your medical records

4. **Informed Consent**
   - Participate in decisions about your care
   - Accept or refuse any treatment, medication, or procedure
   - Be informed before any experimental treatment
   - Sign consent forms only after understanding implications
   - Withdraw consent at any time

5. **Second Opinion**
   - Seek consultation from another physician
   - Request referral to specialists
   - No penalty for seeking second opinion

6. **Quality Care**
   - Receive appropriate medical care based on your condition
   - Access to necessary healthcare services
   - Be informed of hospital policies affecting your care
   - Safe and clean environment

7. **Complaint & Grievance**
   - Voice complaints without fear of reprisal
   - Receive prompt investigation and response
   - Contact patient relations officer
   - File formal grievance if unsatisfied

8. **Financial Information**
   - Receive itemized bill with clear explanation
   - Understand payment policies before treatment
   - Know about insurance coverage
   - Request financial counseling if needed

### Patient Responsibilities

To ensure quality care and a safe environment, patients are expected to:

1. **Provide Accurate Information**
   - Give complete and honest medical history
   - Inform about medications, allergies, and supplements
   - Update changes in health status
   - Report previous treatments and surgeries
   - Provide accurate insurance and personal information

2. **Follow Treatment Plans**
   - Follow prescribed treatment and medication schedule
   - Ask questions if instructions are unclear
   - Inform doctor if unable to follow treatment plan
   - Keep follow-up appointments

3. **Respect Healthcare Providers**
   - Treat doctors, nurses, and staff with courtesy
   - Be on time for appointments
   - Cancel appointments if unable to attend
   - Follow hospital rules and regulations

4. **Financial Obligations**
   - Fulfill financial obligations promptly
   - Provide insurance information accurately
   - Inform about inability to pay
   - Seek financial counseling if needed

5. **Safety & Security**
   - Follow safety instructions
   - Inform staff of safety concerns
   - Do not bring valuables to hospital
   - Not smoke anywhere in hospital premises
   - Not consume alcohol or prohibited substances

6. **Respect for Others**
   - Respect privacy and rights of other patients
   - Maintain reasonable noise levels
   - Follow visiting hours
   - Not take photos/videos of other patients or staff

## Privacy & Data Protection Policy

### Confidentiality

Apollo Medicity Hospital is committed to protecting patient information:

**What We Protect:**
- Medical history and records
- Diagnostic reports and test results
- Treatment plans and prescriptions
- Personal identification information
- Insurance and financial information
- Photographs and videos taken for medical purposes

**Who Can Access:**
- Healthcare providers directly involved in your care
- Staff necessary for billing and administrative purposes
- Authorized insurance representatives
- Legal authorities with proper documentation
- Persons you specifically authorize in writing

**Access Restrictions:**
- Information shared on need-to-know basis only
- Electronic records password-protected
- Physical records kept in secure locations
- Staff trained in confidentiality protocols
- Violation of confidentiality leads to disciplinary action

### Data Protection

In compliance with Information Technology Act, 2000 and Personal Data Protection Bill:

**Data Collection:**
- Only necessary information collected
- Purpose explained at time of collection
- Consent obtained before collection

**Data Usage:**
- Used only for medical care, billing, and legal requirements
- Not sold or shared with third parties for marketing
- Anonymized for research and quality improvement

**Data Security:**
- Electronic systems encrypted and firewall-protected
- Regular security audits conducted
- Backup systems for data recovery
- Access logs maintained

**Patient Rights:**
- Request copy of your data
- Request correction of inaccurate data
- Request deletion of data (subject to legal requirements)
- Know how your data is being used

### Exceptions to Confidentiality

Information may be disclosed without consent in:
- Medical emergencies to save life
- Court orders and legal proceedings
- Suspected child abuse or neglect
- Infectious disease reporting (as per law)
- Suspected criminal activity

## Consent Policy

### Types of Consent

**1. General Consent (At Admission)**
- Routine examinations and procedures
- Blood tests and diagnostic tests
- Nursing care
- Photography for medical records

**2. Informed Consent (For Specific Procedures)**
Required for:
- Surgeries and invasive procedures
- Anesthesia
- Blood transfusion
- Chemotherapy/radiation
- Experimental treatments or clinical trials

**Informed Consent Includes:**
- Nature and purpose of procedure
- Expected benefits
- Material risks and complications
- Alternative treatment options
- Consequences of refusing treatment
- Opportunity to ask questions

**3. Emergency Consent**
- In life-threatening emergencies, treatment may proceed without written consent
- Consent obtained from next of kin if available
- Decision made by two senior doctors
- Documented in medical records

### Who Can Give Consent

**Adults (18+ years):**
- Patient themselves if mentally competent

**Minors (Under 18):**
- Parent or legal guardian
- In absence, another relative (in emergency)

**Mentally Incompetent Adults:**
- Legal guardian
- Next of kin
- Court-appointed representative

### Withdrawal of Consent

- Can be withdrawn at any time orally or in writing
- Doctor will explain consequences
- Will be documented in medical record
- Does not affect right to future care

### Special Situations

**Refusal of Blood Transfusion:**
- Respect for religious beliefs
- Written refusal required
- Risks explained and documented
- Alternative treatments offered if available

**Do Not Resuscitate (DNR):**
- Patient or family can request DNR
- Must be in writing
- Requires two doctors' signatures
- Can be revoked at any time
- Does not mean withdrawal of other care

## Billing & Refund Policy

### Payment Policy

**Advance Deposit:**
- Required at admission (non-refundable administrative charge may apply)
- Amount varies by treatment type
- Adjusted against final bill
- Excess refunded after discharge

**Interim Bills:**
- Generated every 3-5 days for long admissions
- Payment expected within 24 hours
- Treatment may be affected by non-payment

**Final Bill:**
- Prepared after discharge order
- Must be settled before leaving hospital
- Detailed breakdown provided

**Emergency Treatment:**
- Initial stabilization before payment discussion
- Advance deposit required once stable

### Payment Methods

Accepted:
- Cash
- Credit/Debit cards (Visa, Mastercard, RuPay)
- UPI and digital wallets
- Net banking
- Cheque (with prior approval)
- Insurance (cashless or reimbursement)
- Corporate credit (for tie-up companies)

Not Accepted:
- Foreign currency (exchange at ATM/bank)
- Postdated cheques

### Insurance Claims

**Cashless Treatment:**
- Inform at admission
- Pre-authorization obtained from insurer
- Hospital settles directly with insurance company
- Patient pays non-covered items and co-payment

**Reimbursement:**
- Patient pays hospital
- Hospital provides claim documents
- Patient submits to insurance company
- Insurance reimburses patient directly

**Hospital's Role:**
- Assist in claim documentation
- Provide required certificates
- Not responsible for claim rejection
- Not liable for insurance policy terms

### Refund Policy

**Eligible for Refund:**
- Excess advance deposit
- Cancelled procedures (if cancellation policy met)
- Duplicate payments
- Overcharges (after verification)

**Refund Process:**
- Request at billing counter with original receipt
- Verification: 3-5 working days
- Refund mode same as payment mode
- Cash refunds: Up to ₹10,000
- Higher amounts: Cheque/bank transfer (7-10 working days)

**Non-Refundable:**
- Administrative charges
- Consumables already used
- Registration fees
- Emergency consultation fees (even if not admitted)

**Cancellation Policy:**
- Surgery cancellation 48+ hours before: 75% refund
- Surgery cancellation 24-48 hours before: 50% refund
- Surgery cancellation less than 24 hours: No refund
- Emergency surgery: No cancellation allowed

### Disputed Bills

If you dispute a bill:
1. Raise concern at billing counter
2. Request itemized breakdown
3. Speak with billing supervisor
4. File written complaint to Finance Department
5. Response within 5 working days
6. If unresolved, escalate to Patient Relations Officer

**Note:** Payment of undisputed portion expected while investigation ongoing.

### Financial Assistance

For economically challenged patients:
- Contact Social Services Department
- Submit income proof and documents
- Committee reviews on case-by-case basis
- Partial waiver or payment plan may be offered
- Available for Indian residents only

## Visitor & Attendant Policy

### Visitor Guidelines

**General Wards:**
- Maximum 2 visitors at a time
- Visiting hours: 11 AM - 1 PM and 4 PM - 7 PM
- Duration: 30 minutes per visit

**Private Rooms:**
- Maximum 2-3 visitors at a time
- Extended hours: 10 AM - 8 PM
- More flexible timing

**ICU/CCU:**
- Only 1 visitor per visit
- Two visiting slots: 11 AM and 5 PM (10 minutes each)
- Immediate family only

**Pediatric Ward:**
- Parents allowed 24/7
- One parent can stay overnight
- Other visitors: 2 maximum during regular hours

**Maternity Ward:**
- Spouse allowed most times
- Other visitors during regular hours only

### Visitor Restrictions

**Who Cannot Visit:**
- Children under 12 years (in ICU/CCU)
- Persons with infectious diseases
- Intoxicated individuals
- Persons displaying aggressive behavior

**What Visitors Cannot Do:**
- Sit on patient beds
- Use patient washrooms
- Touch medical equipment
- Disturb other patients
- Take photos/videos without permission
- Bring outside food (without doctor approval)
- Smoke or consume alcohol

### Attendant Policy

**Who Can Stay:**
- One attendant per patient (general/semi-private ward)
- Immediate family member preferred
- ID proof required

**Attendant Facilities:**
- Separate bed in private rooms
- Shared facilities in general wards
- Meals at subsidized rates
- Washroom access

**Attendant Responsibilities:**
- Assist with patient's non-medical needs
- Follow hospital rules
- Not interfere with medical treatment
- Maintain cleanliness
- Respect other patients

## Safety & Security Policy

### Patient Safety

**Infection Control:**
- Regular sanitization of wards
- Hand hygiene mandatory for all
- Isolation protocols for infectious diseases
- Antibiotic stewardship program
- Mandatory masks in specified areas

**Fall Prevention:**
- Bed rails for high-risk patients
- Call bells within reach
- Non-slip flooring
- Assistance for mobility

**Medication Safety:**
- Double-check by nurses before administration
- Patient identification verified
- Adverse reactions monitored
- Medication reconciliation at admission/discharge

**Fire Safety:**
- Fire extinguishers on every floor
- Emergency exits clearly marked
- Regular fire drills conducted
- Evacuation plan displayed

### Security Measures

**Access Control:**
- Visitor passes issued at reception
- Bag checking at entrance
- CCTV surveillance throughout
- Security personnel 24/7

**Prohibited Items:**
- Weapons of any kind
- Illegal drugs
- Alcohol
- Flammable materials
- Sharp objects (except medical)

**Valuables:**
- Hospital not responsible for lost valuables
- Safe deposit lockers available (limited)
- Attendants should not bring valuables
- Report theft immediately to security

### Emergency Protocols

**Fire Emergency:**
- Alert nearest staff immediately
- Follow evacuation instructions
- Do not use elevators
- Assist patients if safe to do so

**Medical Emergency:**
- Press call button
- Code Blue team responds immediately
- Visitors should leave area unless asked to stay

**Security Emergency:**
- Report to security desk
- Call extension 2222
- Lock doors if advised
- Follow staff instructions

## Infection Control & Hygiene

### Hand Hygiene

Mandatory for:
- All healthcare workers before/after patient contact
- Visitors before entering and after leaving patient areas
- Patients before meals and after using washroom

Facilities:
- Hand sanitizer dispensers throughout hospital
- Soap and water in all washrooms
- Disposable towels available

### Visitor Restrictions During Outbreaks

During infectious disease outbreaks:
- Visitor numbers may be reduced
- Additional screening at entrance
- Mandatory masks for all
- Specific visiting hours may change
- Children may be restricted

### Isolation Precautions

For patients with infectious diseases:
- Private room isolation
- Restricted visitors
- Special precautions for healthcare workers
- Discharge only after clearance

## Complaints & Grievance Redressal

### How to File a Complaint

**Immediate Concerns:**
- Speak to nurse in charge
- Contact floor supervisor
- Call Patient Relations: Extension 2900

**Formal Complaint:**
1. Fill complaint form at reception
2. Submit to Patient Relations Office
3. Or email: complaints@apollomedicity.in
4. Or mail to hospital address

**Information Required:**
- Your name and contact details
- Patient name and registration number
- Date and time of incident
- Details of complaint
- Names of persons involved (if known)
- What resolution you expect

### Complaint Handling Process

1. **Acknowledgment:** Within 24 hours
2. **Investigation:** 2-5 working days
3. **Response:** Within 7 working days
4. **Resolution:** Appropriate action taken
5. **Follow-up:** Satisfaction check

### Escalation

If unsatisfied with response:
1. Escalate to Hospital Administrator
2. Contact Quality Assurance Department
3. Write to hospital management
4. External: State Medical Council
5. Consumer Forum (for billing disputes)

### Anonymous Complaints

- Can be filed anonymously
- May limit our ability to respond directly
- Still investigated thoroughly
- Suggestion boxes available at multiple locations

## Medical Records Policy

### Access to Records

**Patient Rights:**
- Access your complete medical record
- Receive copies (fee applicable)
- Request corrections of errors
- Understand what's in your record

**How to Request:**
- Fill form at Medical Records Department
- Provide valid ID proof
- Pay applicable fees
- Collection in 24-48 hours

**Fees:**
- Photocopy: ₹100 per record
- Sealed/official copy: ₹500
- CD of scans: ₹200
- Notarized copies: ₹1,000

### Record Retention

- Medical records retained for 10 years (as per law)
- X-rays/scans: 5 years
- After retention period: Disposed securely

### Transfer of Records

For referral to another hospital:
- Request medical records department
- Summary prepared by doctor
- Reports and scans provided on CD
- Original prescriptions provided
